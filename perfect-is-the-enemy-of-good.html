Perfect is the enemy of good

14 May 2019

Recently I reviewed all of the error messages for our online service.

This meant reviewing what we already had built and checking for any error scenarios we hadn’t covered, making sure users would see a helpful message if an error occurred.

I spent a long time obsessing over trying to perfect the messages and held onto the work for too long. I lost sight of what was good enough. In reality there is no perfect error message. Even if we found a message wasn’t working, I could still iterate on the message.

Working on these error messages taught me a valuable lesson as a designer to release the value of my work as early as possible. Don’t spend too long trying to make your work perfect. Make it good enough.
